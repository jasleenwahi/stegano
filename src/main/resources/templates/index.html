About

Steganography is the practice of concealing a message, image, or file within another message, image, or file in such a way that it is difficult to detect. The goal of steganography is to hide the existence of the hidden data, rather than to keep the content of the hidden data secret. There are many ways to implement steganography, but some of the most common techniques involve manipulating the least significant bits of the carrier file to embed the hidden message. This approach can be used to hide information in a variety of file types, such as images, audio files, and video files. Steganography has a wide range of applications, from hiding sensitive information in plain sight to embedding metadata in digital media files. However, it can also be used for malicious purposes, such as hiding malware in seemingly innocuous files. As a result, steganography is often used in conjunction with other security measures, such as encryption and digital signatures, to protect against unauthorized access and tampering.
